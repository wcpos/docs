WCPOS runs on the hardware you already have — a browser, desktop app, or mobile device —
and connects to the peripherals a retail counter needs. This page lists every device type
and points you to its setup guide.

## Receipt printers {#receipt-printers}

Connect a thermal or standard receipt printer over the network, Bluetooth, or USB, and
control a connected cash drawer.

→ [Receipt Printers](/hardware/printers)

## Cash drawers {#cash-drawers}

A cash drawer opens via a kick command sent by a connected receipt printer — there's no
separate connection to configure.

→ See [Cash Drawers](/hardware/printers#cash-drawers) on the Receipt Printers page.

## Barcode scanners {#barcode-scanners}

Most USB and Bluetooth barcode scanners work as keyboard input — no driver needed. The POS
detects fast scanner input automatically. Camera scanning is also supported on mobile.

→ Set up scanning in [Barcode Scanning](/pos/product-panel/barcode-scanning), and tune
detection in [Barcode Settings](/settings/store/barcode).

## Card readers & payment terminals {#card-readers}

Card readers (Stripe Terminal, SumUp, Vipps MobilePay) pair as part of setting up their
payment gateway — the account connection and device pairing are one task.

→ [Payment Gateways](/payment/gateways)

## Tablets & devices {#tablets-devices}

WCPOS runs on the web, the desktop apps (Windows/macOS/Linux), and the mobile apps
(iOS/Android).

→ See [Installation](/getting-started/installation) for supported platforms and the
[Connect](/getting-started/connect) guide for the desktop and mobile apps.
